# Borough Laws

## 1. Borough legislature

#### Local committees

* **1.1** Each committee larger than 10 people will be broken up into groups of 5-7 people who will each vote for a speaker and discuss each issue amongst themselves before speakers get together in groups to relay their groups opinions. If there are more than 10 chosen speakers, they will also be broken up into groups of 5-7 in the same way and the group of speakers and the groups that chose them will then choose upper level speakers. This process will continue until there are 10 or fewer top-level speakers.
  * *Justification: This gives everyone the easy ability to voice their opinion while still allowing efficient group communication.*

## 2. Borough court

* **2.1** Borough court will be structured according to the constitutional provisions for city court.
* **2.2** Initiating suit. To bring suit, a plaintiff must go through the following steps:
  1. Declare the case to the borough's court, identifying the plaintiff, the case, and a description or identification of the defendant. 
     * *Justification: the identity of the defendant may not be known until they acknowledge receipt of the case.*
  2. The information of the case declaration must be served to the defendant by A. direct means, meaning in person or by any means of communication where receipt of the information can be confirmed, B. indirect means, meaning any reliable means of communication where the defendant is known to receive and read or respond, or C. public means, meaning any public means of communication likely to be seen by the defendant or people who know the defendant. The person who served the case declaration must file an affidavit with the borough court documenting the service. 
  3. The defendant must acknowledge receipt of the case declaration with the borough court. If the defendant has been A. served by direct means and has not acknowledged receipt within 30 days of being served, or B. served by indirect means and at least  50 days after being served is served by public means and then has not acknowledged receipt 50 days after public service, then the plaintiff may go ahead with the case without a defense if and only if the expected remedies include some significant remedies that do not require the presence of the defendant to enforce. 
  4. 
     Next, if the defendant's identity is known, the private code of law is chosen according to what codes of law the plaintiff and defendant have opted into. The case then continues according to that code of law. If the defendant's identity is not known, the case then continues according to default borough law. 
* **2.3** If suit is brought up and harm is shown with adequate proof and that harm was being caused for more than the plaintiff, 10%-20% of the total harm-done (up to a maximum of the lesser of $200,000 or 1% of the portion above that amount) is additionally awarded to the plaintiff. All the rest of the restitution collected is distributed to each harmed individual by estimated amount of harm for the 100 most harmed people, and if there are more than 100, the rest will receive even portions. This includes in cases where a large fraction of a borough is harmed (eg pollution). In cases where harmed individuals can be counted but not identified, their portion of compensation will be returned to the residents of the borough evenly.
  * *Justification: This provides an incentive for private individuals to investigate and prove harm caused to others that might otherwise be too costly to enforce on an individual basis.*
* **2.4** Any suit brought officially but settled out of court must have the settlement made public.
* Holding facilities
  * **2.5** Holding facilities will be paid out for prison vouchers in the following way:
    * A recidivism score will be calculated each year for each relevant holding facility. Each former-prisoner is given a recidivism score equal to the total amount of prison time they are sentenced to in the 4 years following release from a holding facility will be divided by the time of the prison sentences they were serving in the previous 4 years. The score for the holding facility will be the sum of score for each former-prisoner. 
    * 50% of a prison voucher will be paid out on a monthly basis as time is served. The other 50% is reserved for 4 years. The holding facility will be paid an amount equal to the summed value of all vouchers that come due in a given year multiplied by `(ARR/HFRR - 0.5)/1.5` with a minimum of 0% and maximum of 100%, where
      * `ARR` is the average recidivism score for all holding facilities for the last 4 years,
      * `HFRR` is a given holding facility's recidivism score.
    * *Justification: This formula means that if the holding facility has half the average recidivism rate, it gets the full voucher value, and if it has double the average recidivism rate, it gets nothing from the reserved voucher payouts.*
  * **2.6** If there is no room in a holding facility for a person newly sentenced to confinement or no borough budget to pay for the confinement, the city will prioritize the existing people serving confinement sentences (including this new person) and order the release of the person with the lowest likelihood of causing additional harm, and if there are ties, the person with the least sentence left to serve.
    * *Justification: Eg, if there is only one holding facility that has space upon arrest and space cannot be made in another choice, the arrestee must be released. This would naturally prioritize more serious crimes and reduce overcrowded systems by releasing people with low-seriousness offenses, rather than forcing people to wait overlong for the process to get to them.*
  * **2.7** If a holding facility is for whatever reason no longer able to confine some of the people they are currently holding, those people will be transferred to a different facility. If there is no room or no budget for all of these people, the same process will be used as that for the case where there is no room for a newly sentenced person.
* **2.8** If a borough court decision cannot be enforced because the defendant has left the jurisdiction and is not expected to return within 1 month, the court must grant the plaintiff the right to pursue the defendant in a court at a higher level than the city. If the defendant entered the city without signing the constitution, the city must offer to pursue the defendant in a higher level court for trespass if the defendant is willing to pay the relevant court fees.

## 3. Tax and subsidies

* **3.1** Land externality tax. The land value, not including buildings, improvements, or valuable natural features located on the land, will be taxed at a rate of 95% of the estimated rental value of land. This estimated value will be evaluated on a block by block basis of blocks approximately 200 meters on a side. Any substantial difference in land values within a given block (eg because of particularly valuable views or substantial differences in utility access) will be included in the estimation of individual plots on a case by case basis. 
  * *Justification: A 95% tax is done instead of 100% to give some wiggle room for evaluation error. Near-100% is chosen because an 100% LVT is the most economically efficient rate of LVT.*
* **3.2** Any employer who employs a person receiving income from a universal basic income (UBI) can receive a subsidy by negotiating a rate of pay with their employee below the minimum wage set by a higher level of government. The employer then will receive a monthly subsidy payment equal to the relevant minimum wage minus the amount negotiated with the employee up to the amount that the employee would earn via the UBI. That employee's UBI is then reduced by the amount of subsidy the employer received.
  * *Justification: Minimum wage neutralization. This gives businesses an opportunity to comply with minimum wage law while not disincentivizing employment of workers whose skills are less valuable than minimum wage, albeit only in the case that the employee would receive UBI and only to the degree that UBI can fund the subsidy.*
* **3.3** Publicly fund a voucher of $4000/year (2023) per minor ages 6-18 which may be used at any educational institution. 
  * *Justification: An educated population convers a positive externality onto the town in the form of smarter and more efficient actions and businesses.*
* **3.4** Publicly fund a voucher of $1000/year (2023) per minor ages 0-6 which may be used at any child supervisory institution.
  * *Justification: Child rearing also confers a positive externality onto the town by supplying fresh minds and social partners to foster a healthy continuing community. Therefore these things should be rewarded with a subsidy.*
* **3.5** Publicly fund a voucher of $300/month (2023) per unmarried person ages 18-35. 
  * This will be made available to women starting when the fraction of unmarried men to unmarried women in the borough in the 18-35 age range exceeds 52% for two consecutive years and ends when that fraction drops below 48%.
  * This will be made available to men starting when the fraction of unmarried men to unmarried women in the borough in the 18-35 age range drops below 48% for two consecutive years and ends when that fraction exceeds 52%.
  * *Justification: An area with an imbalance of men and women causes many societal problems that affect everyone, thus causing a negative externality. Places with too many men, for example, have more anti-social behavior, crime (especially violent crime), and suicide.*
* **3.6** Subsidy for public ways and public space based on usage.
  * Reward freely usable public spaces with a subsidy of $1 per person who passed into the public space per day.
    * *Justification: Compensates private production of public spaces, eg rooftop parks, elevated gardenways between buildings, public space on an upper floor within a building, etc.*
* **3.7** Subsidy for each insurance monopolex based on production of positive externalities:
  1. Collect information on monopolex insurance that people buy and estimate a positive externality of each of them.
  2. Distribute a subsidy to everyone that has relevant insurance that confers a positive externality based on the amount each contributes.
* **3.8** Hold a contest each year to determine and reward properties and people producing the most positive externalities.
  * Nominations can be submitted the entire year. A person or property may not be nominated twice within a 3 year period.
  * A score vote will be had where each resident and land owner give each nomination a score of the total amount they think that nomination should receive as compensation for producing their positive externalities. A voters scores in total may not exceed the yearly budget. Nominees with positive externalities already covered by subsidies in other ways must have their existing subsidies and subsidy amounts clearly enumerated on the ballot, with the instruction that voters should exclude those from the amount they vote to award them.
  * Half the budget is allocated by counting the votes by each of the weightings (resident weighting vs land value weighting).
  * The budget is allocated to a nominee is allocated by dividing that nominees total score by the sum of total scores of all nominees. Each nominee receives the allocated budget over 4 years, being awarded 1/3rd of the budget the first 2 years, and 1/6th the budget the 2nd two years. 
  * *Justification: Allows people to identify and select producers of positive externalities to be rewarded for the benefits they provide the community. This might include producers of public good information, beautiful buildings, gardens, etc.*
* **3.9** Any excess collected taxes will be used to reimburse state and federal taxes in the following ways, prioritized based on how much deadweight loss is estimated to be relieved, the largest first.
  1. State property tax will be deducted from a plot's land value tax at an equal percentage per plot.
  2. State sales tax will be reimbursed at an equal percentage per sale up to 50% for all sales that happen within the borough. Sales taxes for sales to city residents are reimbursed at double that percentage. 
  3. Federal personal income taxes and payroll taxes for borough residents will be reimbursed at an equal percentage per person up to 50%. Income taxes and payroll taxes earned for income activity that happens within the city borders is reimbursed at double that percentage.
     * *Note that this will incentivize people to use businesses with pass-through taxes, which keeps the tax code simpler while still allowing methods for businesses to take advantage of tax reimbursement.*

## 4. Land rights

Land held by the borough will have, when sold, the following Land rights for the purchaser:

* **4.1** Right to exclude others from causing matter or energy, of a kind or quantity that doesn't usually or naturally occur on that land, to enter their land. 
* **4.2** Right to prevent others from reducing matter or energy of a kind that usually and naturally occurs on that land from entering that land by more than 10%, excepting flows that naturally cross many property boundaries (like water). 
    * *This needs to consider airflow rights, sunlight and indirect light rights, and rainfall collection rights. But I don't know how to best define this.*
* **4.3** Right to prevent others from reducing flows that usually and naturally occur (like water flows) from entering that land by more than 1% of the natural upstream flow per mile.
* **4.4** The owner of land owns the space above it up to 70 feet above the ground at the property line, with the height limit increasing 10 feet for every 1 foot of distance from the property line. The owner also owns 200 feet below the ground to the extent that doing so doesn't damage natural cave systems at least partially owned by a different owner or that doing so doesn't violate the previous two rights (about matter and energy), for example by disrupting the water table. 
* Properties are allowed an arbitrary choice of 8x8 square per quarter-acre they can build on with no height limit. This right is not natively usable by properties less than a quarter-acre, but the fractional right is transferrable. 
    * Perhaps the last two rules can be generalized by allowing any  building above the height limit as long as it blocks less than an average of X area of light per hour of daylight within a 400 meter radius. This would open the door for all sorts of interesting spires, causeways, and other things to be experimented. For example, you could imagine a building on stilts elevated 100 meters in the air serving as additional space on the property without blocking much light during the day. 
* Properties are allowed to build parts of buildings that overhang their adjacent public way such that a maximum area of the width of the public way multiplied by 5% of its length has overhangs above it. These rights are transferrable to anyone else within 600 feet on a given public way. 
    * This allows buildings to have connections across public ways or overhanging rooms.
* **4.5** Discovered natural cave systems that span below multiple properties are jointly owned by the property owners of the land above them where each owner has a fractional ownership proportional to the fraction of total cave volume. Decisions impacting the cave system must be made with a 60% vote of the owner proportional to their ownership fractions. Ownership fractions can be sold. 
* **4.6** Land rights include the right and responsibility of block stewardship for adjacent public ways as listed in the borough constitution.
* Land may be placed into one of the following height-zoning categories that modify the default land rights:
    * **4.7** Mid-rise zone - Default land rights apply with the modification that the owner owns the space above the ground up to 300 feet, and any part of the building above that height must be set back from the property line by 1 foot for every 40 feet above 300 feet.
    * **4.8** High-rise zone - Default land rights apply with the modification that the owner owns the space above the ground up to 700 feet, and any part of the building above that height must be set back from the property line by 1 foot for every 80 feet above 700 feet.
    * **4.10** Skyscraper zone - Default land rights apply with the modification that the owner owns the space above the ground up to 2000 feet and can build a structure of any height without limit, at which point the space up to the highest point of the structure plus 100 feet becomes fully owned by the owner.
* Land may be placed into one of the following externality-zoning categories intended to allow for different standards for equivalences of harm.
    * **4.11** Commercial zone - Intended to allow mildly elevated externalities that some commercial or entertainment businesses generate.
    * **4.12** Industrial zone - Intended to allow significantly elevated externalities that some industrial businesses generate.

## 5. Law of guardians and wards

### Guardianship status
**5.1** The women who gives birth to a child is presumed to be the mother and guardian of that child unless a prior contract exists for carrying a and giving birth to the baby on behalf of someone else.

**5.2** The man in an exclusive sexual relationship with the mother around the time of conception of the child is presumed to be the father and guardian of the child if there are no objections from the mother or father. If there are objections, convincing evidence that the child was created using genetic material from the father establishes presumed fatherhood and guardian status outside the context of a related prior contract for use of that genetic material. A man may be released of the duty of fatherhood and guardianship of the child if he makes his unwillingness to be a father clearly known at least 3 weeks before legal and medically safe cancelation of the pregnancy becomes no longer possible, but the pregnant woman in that situation is entitled to damages for the emotional, mental, and physical stress and pain, financial burden, burden of time lost, and opportunity costs of the pregnancy and consideration of cancelation.

**5.3** At age 16, unless the person is still determined to be incapable of making adequate decisions for their own well being, they cease to have guardians and are a legal adult.  

**5.4** Any person can become a guardian or be relieved from being guardian of a ward with the consent of all other guardians and the person whose guardianship status is being changed. The ward must also give consent for adding a guardian if they are capable of understanding the consequences of the decision within 3 weeks of the guardians agreeing on the decision. A guardian being added by existing guardians may be given a contracted subset of rights and responsibilities.

**5.5** A court can order to revoke someone's guardianship status, and may replace them with other obligations and rights (eg child support obligations or visitation rights) if it finds that the guardian no longer able to properly see to the well being of the ward.

**5.6** A court may decide to remove the guardianship relationship for a person above the age of 14 who no longer wants guardians and the court determines that the person is able to properly care for themselves and productively interact in society without any guardian. 

**5.7** Any person without a guardian determined to be incapable of making adequate decisions for their own well being may be given guardians by a court, with the consent of the prospective guardians, if it finds the prospective guardians able to give the dependent the care and guidance they need. Consent of the prospective guardians may be determined unnecessary if there is no practical alternative guardian and the guardians have a close social or biological relationship with the prospective ward. The wishes of the prospective ward must be respected to the degree they are congruous with the wellbeing of the ward.

**5.8** A deceased person without a pre-specified guardian may also be given guardians by a court for the purposes of resolving their outstanding estate.

### Responsibilities of a guardian
**5.9** To ensure the physical needs of the ward to maintain their full health are met or, if the guardian's physical needs are not fully met, ensure the physical needs of the ward are met at least as well as they themselves are nourished. This primarily includes hydration, nutrition, sleep, shelter, physical safety, and safety against psychological harm. 

**5.10** To ensure the healthy physical and mental development of the ward, to the degree reasonable for the ward's status.

**5.11** To give the ward autonomy in making choices for themselves when they are capable of doing so.

**5.12** To safeguard assets given to the ward.

**5.13** The guardians take responsibility for any court ordered remedies for harm caused by the ward. The guardians can choose to spread the burden of such remedies amongst the guardians and the ward in question. 

* *For example, if confinement is ordered by a court, one or more guardians can choose to serve all or part of the sentence in place of the ward.*

**5.13** Unless specified on initiation or change of status of a guardian, guardians share equal responsibilities. 

### Rights of a guardian
A guardian has the right to:

**5.14** Make decisions for the ward as their agent. 
**5.15** Discipline the ward in ways that do not do significant harm or inflict significant pain on the ward.

### Rights of a ward

**5.16** A ward age 14 or older of normal mental development has autonomy over their own body as far as making decisions about natural bodily functions, including sex, and refraining from putting unnatural things in their body (such as medicine or medical devices).

**5.17** Minors may create contracts, but must have guardian consent for any contracts that require a high cost (time, money, etc), long-term obligations, or potential danger to the minor. A high cost is acceptable for immediate life necessities as long as the cost is a justifiably fair market price. Length of time and amount of cost both scale up with age (**how?**). 

**5.18** A person must be mentally competent at the time of contract contract acceptance and must appear mentally competent to the contract offeror without reasonable doubt. Otherwise their guardian must also agree to the contract. While the offeror has a responsibility to investigate reasonable doubt of mental incompetence, even with no outward signs of mental incompetence a contract may be voidable if it can be shown that the acceptor was mentally incompetent at the time of acceptance and did not explicitly or implicitly affirm the acceptance at a later time of lucidity. In cases of negligent contracting, the contract is void. In such cases of unexpected incompetence, the harmed party may be awarded no more than 10% of the compensation that would result from a competent counterparty.

## 6. Law of organizations
### Requirements
**6.1** The organization must be given a clear name that is used in all contracts the organization participates in.
**6.2** Owners of the organization must be specified among which final decision making is vested and to which all assets would be distributed in the case of dissolution of the organization. 

### Attributes
**6.3** The owners, in their role as owners, may, in the articles of incorporation of the organization, be specified as being personally excluded from liability for any contractual obligations of the organization. In the case of the organization failing to fulfill contractual obligations, only assets of the organization itself may be allocated to pay for damages. 
**6.4** Owners may be held responsible for non-contractual damage or bad faith contractual damage if their organizational decisions directly caused the damage. If the damage was not directly caused by the decisions of the owners, then the agents of the organization may be held personally responsible to the degree that their actions caused harm.

## 7. Insurance monopolexes

Insurance monopolexes. Each of the following are separate insurance monopolexes that the borough pays for as specified in the borough constitution:

* **7.1** Prison sentence insurance - Insurance that compensates the borough for the costs of imprisoning offenders and compensates victims for part of the damages to them that they can't recover from the defendant. 
* **7.2** Personal property rights violation insurance - Insurance that compensates a person for harm done to them that cannot be recovered from the offender. This should include any punitive damages owed.
* **7.3** External fire insurance - Insurance that pays out on fire damage caused by a fire that started on a property not owned by the insured person. 
* **7.4** Flood insurance
* **7.5** Emergency health insurance - Insurance that covers individuals from damages caused by health hazards in the borough.
  * *Justification: Incentivizes a private consortium public health and safety.*
* **7.6** Littering and cleanliness insurance - This is insurance that pays out depending on how dirty a covered location is.
  * *Justification: Incentivizes private insurance consortiums to create programs for preventing littering and promoting cleanliness.*

# 8. Borough limitations

* **8.1** All monetary amounts will be written as inflation adjusted values based on CPI. 
* **8.2** Monetary expenditures in city and borough law must be prioritized. (See recommended city laws for details).

Limitations expressed as rights and freedoms of private persons:

* **8.3** Right to privacy when accused. This is a right to be able to choose to allow release of information about the dispute or to keep it private to only necessary parties until the dispute is resolved.
  * *Justification: This can keep active dispute from being unfairly used by 3rd parties against a person to assume something other than innocence in their life.*

# 9. Visitation, residency, and expulsion

* **9.1** All people who owned land within the borough before or concurrent to the formation of the city have Permanent Access status.
* **9.2** For a person to gain Trial Access status, three people with Permanent Access must vouch for the person. 
* **9.3** For someone to gain Permanent Access status, they must first have Trial Access status. At the end of a 1 year trial period, a hearing is held where people may come to voice their opinions about giving the candidate Permanent Access. 
  * **9.3.1** In the hearing, a discussion will happen for up to 1 hour until someone calls for a vote and those with Permanent Access present vote with a 70% majority for discussion to end. At the end of 1 hour, a 70% vote may be called to extend the discussion for another 1 hour, with a maximum of 3 extensions totaling a maximum of 4 hours. 
  * **9.3.2** After the initial discussion, a questioning session will happen with the same times and finality conditions as the initial discussion. Those with Permanent Access present will ask the candidate questions during this time. 
  * **9.3.3** After the questioning session, a second discussion session will happen with the same times and finality conditions as the initial discussion. 
  * **9.3.4** After the second discussion session, each person with Permanent Access present gives their opinion as either "approve", "extend trial", or "oppose". Those who live within 400 feet of the candidate's residence have double weight in their vote. 
    * The person is given Permanent Access if they receive at least 6 approvals and and at least 75% more approvals than the combined counts for "disapproval" and "extend trial".
    * The person is given renewed Trial Access if they fail to meet the requirement for Permanent Access and their combined "approve" and "extend trial" counts are at least 75% greater than disapprovals.
    * The person has their Trial Access status revoked if they fail to meet either of these criteria and may not be given Trial Access again for 2 years.
* **9.4** For a person to gain Guest Access status, one person with Permanent Access must vouch for the person. 
* **9.5** If more than 4 people with Permanent Access have filed complaints about the person with Guest Access in the last year, the requirement for their renewal of Guest Access increases to a number of people with Permanent Access that is double the number of people with Permanent Access who have filed complaints about them. 
* **9.7** If 10 people file complaints about the person, they must be the subject of a hearing of the same form as is used for evaluating giving Permanent Access to someone who has Trial Access. This hearing will discuss expulsion of the person in question. The votes will be "expel" or "do not expel". If the expulsion is passed their Access will be revoked and they will have 2 months to vacate the borough and must sell any real estate in the borough within 2 years.

## 10. Limitations of residents

**1.2** No resident may wager more than $300 (2023) on any particular election outcome in an election betting pool.

* *Justification: While election betting pools can add an incentive for people to learn about election issues, if too much money is riding on something, it might encourage fraud.*
