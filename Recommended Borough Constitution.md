# Borough government

This is a recommended constitution for one borough in the federation of boroughs within the city. 

## Summary

*This summary is non-normative*

* No substantial bureaucracy. The executive branch primarily functions as an administrator that manages  and directs finances.
* Tragedy of the commons and the free rider problem is solved in two ways:
  * via the borough government subsidizing insurance that cause positive externalities, and
  * via rewarding private individuals who successfully identify and prove harm.
* Natural monopolies and similar situations where healthy competition doesn't naturally happen is solved in one primary way eg for utilities:
  * sections of utilities are collectively owned by the residents adjacent to each section of utility, and those residents vote to decide what company will operate that section of utility. 

## 1. City council

* **1.A** Council members are selected using [reweighted range voting](https://www.rangevoting.org/RRV.html). The vote weight of each council member is determined by the ratio of the initial score that council member received (before any reweighting) divided by the sum of those initial scores for all winning candidates.
* **1.B** Each council member must live in the borough they were elected in for greater than 50% of the year. 
* **1.C** Council members serve for 4 years, and may not serve more than 2 consecutive terms nor 4 terms in total.

## Powers and limitations

### Legislative branch

**2. General structures:**

There are two legislative bodies: the council and local committees.

* **2.A** The borough council is made up of the same 3 council members the borough elects for city council. 
* **2.B** The local committees are the same as are used for city legislative actions.
* **2.C** The rules for the borough legislative bodies are the same as for the city's, excepting the differing legislative powers.

**3. Structure of local committees:**

* **3.A** Each block section of city designated travel way will have a local committee that officially meets on self-determined days. Residents and owners of property bordering the relevant block's public way may join meetings.
* **3.B** Each resident and owner may only vote for city and borough issues as part of a local committee for 1 block. 
* **3.C** Any given local committee may make official decisions on no more than 7 days per year.
* **3.D** Votes are done in liquid democracy fashion where each person can register a delegate with the borough government for each of a number of categories. In the case they do not vote, or vote to explicitly delegate their vote for a particular issue or ballot, their relevant vote plus any votes delegated to them will be given to their specified delegate. Issues will all be labeled with a category which will determine how to delegate. The following categories can be used:
  * Representatives - This is a vote for government officials. 
  * Property rights
  * Courts and police
  * Land use
  * Taxes and externalities
* **3.E** Any vote to take an official action during official meeting days is not final until either 1 week has passed or a ballot is made available to everyone who did not attend or could not vote in the meeting with the option to place their votes on the proposals passed by attending citizens.

**4. Limitations to Powers:**

All powers and limitations designated for boroughs in the City Constitution are available with the following addendums:

* **4.A** Laws defining property rights, contract law, equivalences of harm, and dispute resolution requirements may only be enacted by selecting a registered private legal code.
* **4.B** Zones may only have differentiated laws for the following:
  * Energy and matter emitted into or blocked from entering a public space or into a 3rd party's private space.
  * Building height limits.
    * *Its likely that this can be expressed in a way that matches the above description about energy and matter, eg light being blocked from adjacent properties. Consider removing this provision in favor of requiring the zoning limitation to be expressed in that kind of form.*
* **4.C** Change in zoning laws or zones must compensate land owners for any land value lost by the change in zoning. 
* **4.D** A preponderance of evidence must show that a law is expected to benefit society more than it costs society before it can be enacted into law.
* **4.E** Laws must be given success criteria, and those criteria will be evaluated after 5 years in a review by a designated borough court. If the criteria is found by the court to have not been achieved, the law must then immediately be scheduled to be repealed within 3 months after that decision.
* **4.F** No law may be made that rewards or punishes any particular procedure, technology, construction, or action by a private citizen or organization. Instead, laws may only reward or punish the material results of one of these things in terms of how they affect others. Defaults rewards or punishments can be legislated for cases where there are ambiguities in how to measure the appropriate reward or punishment. 
  * *Justification: Laws that ban or require specific procedures and the like become out of date very quickly and result in perpetual suboptimal law and intractability of keeping law up to date.*
* **4.G** No law may make exceptions for any particular procedure, technology, construction, action, intention, or role when determining amount of reward or punishment. 
  * *Justification: Too often our society let's harm be done for things that are "necessary" or "reasonable" when what that means is that certain categories of activity have no incentive to limit the harm they impose on others.*
* **4.H** No revenue will be used by the borough except for taxes and borrowed funds. Any property seizure, fines, or anything else must be either destroyed or sold at public auction, with the proceeds of which distributed either to a party owed compensation relevant to the fee or evenly to the people of the borough. 
* **4.I** No money may be borrowed if tax revenue can cover the cost of expenditures.
* **4.J** To take advantage of any welfare program created, a person must have been a resident of the borough for at minimum 1 year and must have been a resident of the borough within the last year.
  * *Justification: this limits welfare to people who have been in (and presumably contributed to) the community for some time already, and limits the risk of some people coming in to take advantage of the welfare and then taking those earnings out of the community.*
  * *I'm thinking it might be actually good to entice people to come to the town and try it out. So maybe don't have this limitation? Not sure.*
* **4.K** Any left over land value tax revenue will be deposited in a reserve fund. If the reserve fund exceeds 2 times the tax revenue collected the prior year, then remaining left over land value tax will be distributed equally to citizens as a citizens dividend. 
  * *Justification: Some use of excess funds should be specified, but it is not recommended that land value taxes be reduced substantially from their ideal 100%, because doing so increases deadweight losses. Finding ways to reimburse the producers of positive externalities would be a better use of funds. A citizens' dividend *
* The following regulatory methods of correcting positive and negative externalities are the only ones allowed:
  * **4.L** Vouchers given under certain condition that can be used to pay for private services in certain contexts when the use of the services can credibly be shown to produce a net positive externality of at least the amount the voucher is given for. 
  * **4.M** Subsidies based on metrics that can be credibly used to estimate net positive externality produced. 
  * **4.N** Taxes based on metrics that can be credibly used to estimate net negative externalities produced.
  * **4.O** Land value taxes based on metrics that can be credibly used to estimate net positive externalities conferred onto the land.
  * **4.P** Contests where the public at large expresses preferences on what parties to reward for positive externalities conferred and/or how much to reward each. 
  * **4.Q** Reimbursing taxes from levels of government above the city level in the form of direct payments or in the form of tax reductions. 
* The following regulatory methods of ensuring healthy market competition are the only ones allowed:
  * **4.R** Subsidizing a second competitor for several markets as specified in the city constitution.
  * **4.S** Block stewardship monopolex:
    * Stewardship of each segment of public ways is given collectively to the owners of land bordering to the public way, even if the bordering property is part of a different borough. The public ways and any utilities on the public ways will be operated by one or more contracted companies. Stewardship is weighted based on the total linear length of land bordering the way. 
      * Periodically, prospective operators submit proposals including a secret bids to operate some aspect of the public way, the stewards then score vote on which proposal they want to take. The operator then has the right to operate and charge for services according to the proposal, and sell its services to adjacent sections of infrastructure (eg roads or utilities). 
      * The available prices for use by adjacent sections of utility must be the same options as given to the stewards. 
        * *Justification: Doing this minimizes the possibility of the owners of one section setting monopolistic pricing on an adjacent section - if they have to pay that price too, they'll be more fair about the price.*
      * Borough law may specify standards of interoperation to ensure utility sections operated by different businesses can compatibly interact. 
  * **4.T** Park and nature space stewardship monopolex:
    * Operates exactly the same way as the block stewardship monopolex, except stewardship of each designated park or nature space is given to the owners of land within 400 feet of the space. Stewardship is weighted by the area of land within 400 feet of the space (any fraction of a plot of land farther than that is not included).
  * **4.U** Utility monopolex:
    * Utility and infrastructure networks are broken up into sections and competing sections can be barred from having substantially overlapping direct or indirect ownership. Competing sections could then be privately owned and operated. 
    * Borough law may specify standards of interoperation to ensure utility sections operated by different businesses can compatibly interact. Note that because methods and means cannot be rewarded or punished, this means that only standards of compatibility can be required and any standard specification may only be enforced in the case that the relevant competitors can't come to an agreement on a different specification. 
  * **4.V** Insurance monopolex: 
    * Insurance that matches narrowly defined criteria can be required to be beholden to joint decisions by the consortium of insurance providers that have sold insurance of that kind. A 65% vote on a policy can bind the group of insurers to pay for implementation of the policy, proportional to their vote weight. Each insurance provider is given a voting weight depending on the sum of relevant liability of the insurance contracts.
* **4.W** Only the following categories of visitation and residency may be given separate legislative requirements, processes, and privileges:
  * Guest Access: Someone authorized to enter and move freely in the borough for 1 month after being given or renewing Guest Access status. 
  * Trial Access: Someone authorized to enter and move freely in the borough for 1 year after being given Trial Access status.
  * Permanent Access: Someone authorized to enter and move freely in the borough indefinitely.
  * Citizenship: Someone with Permanent Access who is given direct privileges to affect the governance of the city.
* **4.X** Any person expelled from the borough must be given at least 1 month' to personally vacate the borough and must be given at least 1 year to sell any property the law requires them to sell. 

### 5. Judicial branch

**Limitations to Powers:**

In any court case using default borough laws:

* **5.A** The filing party (the plaintiff in non-appeals cases, the party who lost the original case in an appeals case) pays all of the court fees.
* **5.B** 
  * The `L` likelihood of getting caught and convicted in section 7.H of the city constitution will be estimated for each of the following tiers of harm done:
    * 0 to 1/1000th the median yearly income
    * 1/1000th to 1/100ths
    * 1/100ths to 1/20ths
    * 1/20ths to 1/4th
    * 1/4th and up
  * From the likelihoods in these tiers, this can then be modified for the individual circumstances in the ways described in the city constitution. 
* **5.C** When ordering an outcome requiring evidence beyond a reasonable doubt, the `primary assessed harm`  (`PAH`) will be the lower of `DH/L + OB ` or `DH + S/300 * DH^(0.5)` with variables defined as in City Constitution 7.H, and `S` defined as the median salary in the borough. The cost of any punishments to the offender should be as near as feasible to `PAH + F * CF` where `F` is the fraction of the court fees paid by the defendant. If the filing party wins, `F` is set at `(1-(DO / PAH))` where `DO` is the highest formal settlement offer made by the defendant prior to trial. Defendant pays (1-(defendant offer / harm cost)) of the court fees if they lose, (plaintiff offer/ harm cost) of the court fees if they win.
  * *Justification: While the city constitution allows including the "societal cost" in the harm, defendants shouldn't have the cost of any deterrence or included in their fine because as long as the fine is set as listed above, then net cost to society should always be 0, rendering any personal deterrence or surveillance an individual employs strictly a choice that benefits that individual in the form of risk reduction or the like.*
  * *Justification: The power of* `0.5` *above is to allow a higher ratio of punitive component for lower value fines but also to limit how much extra someone can be punished over the damage they caused for higher value harm.*
  * *Justification: Having the plaintiff generally pay most of the court fees incentivizes them to only take a case to court if the cost of adjudication is worth the correction. Having the non-filing party pay, in the case they lose, incentivizes them not to delay the case in an attempt to escape justice. Having the fraction of the court fees the defendant pays, in the case they lose, be related to their highest settlement offer incentivizes the defendant to make fair offers in order to avoid partially reimbursing the plaintiff for the court fees while at the same time not pushing them to accept higher-than-appropriate settlement offers from the plaintiff.*
* **5.D** When ordering an outcome requiring clear and convincing evidence, the court must order an outcome costing an amount equivalent to between 30% and 70% of the amount required for outcomes requiring beyond a reasonable doubt.
  * *Justification: with a lower burden of proof standard, there is a higher likelihood the charged party is innocent, and so the economically efficient damages to charge is necessarily lower than one with a higher burden of proof.*
* **5.E** If clear and convincing evidence is found that the defendant has not caused the claimed harm, the court must order the plaintiffs to pay the defendants 2% of the sought damages. 
* **5.F** If evidence beyond a reasonable doubt is found that the defendant has not caused the claimed harm must order the plaintiffs to pay the defendants 10% of the sought damages. 
  * *Justification: This compensates for the risk of a mistaken verdict conferred on defendants beyond the nominal cost of their defense. [See here for more info](http://www.daviddfriedman.com/Academic/FeudThenandNow.html).*

### 6. Executive branch

**6.A** A borough president will be elected in the same way the city Mayor is elected. The Borough president organizes the implementation of the laws. 

## 7. Limitations on all persons

Policing

* **7.A** Detainment, forcible search, seizure, arrests, and holding at temporary holding facilities and prisons may only be performed by those who
  * A. have completed and passed a relevant training course that fits standards determined by law, and
  * B. record video of the duration of performing any of those actions as well as the reasonable suspicion or probable cause that prompted their use. 

Detainment

* **7.B** No person or property may be detained for longer than 10 minutes.

Holding
* **7.C** No person may be forcibly held longer than 12 hours after arrest, nor 3 hours after being in a holding facility between the hours of 8am and 10pm, unless the appropriate court has affirmed that the person is a flight risk or likely to repeat offense. No person may be held after the appropriate court has disaffirmed these things. 
* **7.D** No property other than weapons may be forcibly held longer than 12 hours after seizure, unless a court has affirmed the probable cause. 
* **7.E** No person may be forcibly held for longer than 1 week after arrest unless a trial has begun for their case. 
* **7.F** No property may be forcibly held for longer than 1 weeks unless a court decision has determined it should be indefinitely seized pending trial or permanently seized. 
* **7.G** Any person forcibly held must have access to communicate with the outside world at least 4 times every 2 hours and at least 1 hour in total. 
* **7.H** Holding facilities must allow private police to enter the holding facility to watch over and protect a client only if that client has verified that they do want them inside. 
* **7.I** Any person forcibly held for longer than 10 hours must be given the ongoing choice to transfer to any holding facility that can be shown to credibly keep those held inside securely, as long as they can pay for the transfer at-cost and can pay the difference in cost between the new facility and the old facility.

Prison
* **7.N** Any person given confinement as a sentence for harm done in the borough will have their confinement sentence re-evaluated by the borough default court and reduced if it exceeds the amount that default borough law allows. After the sentence is possibly reduced, the borough will be responsible for confinement.
* **7.O** Any person given a prison sentence must be given the opportunity to get their property in order (for example moved into storage). 
* **7.P** For any person given a confinement sentence by borough court, the borough government will give that person a voucher with a value equal to at least half the median rate for housing for a single occupant on the private market which may be used to pay for their choice of any holding facility that is acceptable according to law and can be shown to credibly keep those held inside securely. Compensation to the holding facility should be reduced in relation to recidivism rates such that it is profitable for a holding facility to reduce recidivism. A fraction of the voucher will be paid out on a monthly basis for each month of the sentence, and remainder will be paid out after 4 years on the basis of this calculation of compensation reduction. How this compensation reduction is done will be provided for in borough law. 
  * *Justification: This introduces a mechanism of market competition for holding facilities as well as a mechanism to limit inefficient holding costs. The compensation reduction ensures that some private company has a somewhat long term interest in preventative measures that reduce the likelihood and length of incarcerations, ideally by measures that prevent the kind of crime that leads to that punishment.*
