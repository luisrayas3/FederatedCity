# Land Use

## 1. Network of travel ways

* **1.1** The laws that a public way must follow is the borough whose properties have the most total length of border bordering on that public way.
* **1.2** The ways along the border of each borough should be marked clearly (eg with paint) so people know when they're traveling into a new jurisdiction. 
* Minimally required ways:
  * **1.3** A public road must be built approximately every 1600 meters, which allows cars but may  not allow other modes of transportation. 
  * **1.4** At intervals of approximately every 200 meters, public ways must be maintained that support both walking and small low-speed vehicles (eg bicycles) but do not allow large or high-speed vehicles.
  * **1.5** A 200 meter wide  strip of nature-land with walkways must be maintained at intervals of approximately every 1600 meters.

## 2. City limitations

* **2.1** Monetary expenditures in city and borough law must be prioritized into at least 3 levels of priority.
   * First priority items in total may not be expected to use more than 70% of the smallest amount of spendable government tax revenue from the past 5 years. 
   * If items with a given priority cannot be fully funded, items of that priority will be partially and equally funded, and no items of lower priority may be funded.
   * *Justification: This provides a means to gracefully degrade government operation if money is tight.*
